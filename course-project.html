Part 1: Data

The BRFSS study is of observational type where stratified sampling was employed, hence the sampling is random.The whole survey is observational. No causality can be determined from this study. Numerous issues exist with the sampling methods. The use of telephones as the method of contact limits the sample to only those people who have landline telephones and reside in residntial or college housing. Other issue is the interview format. One may doubt the data due to issues such as intelligibilty of the question asked about the health or whether memory issues are biasing results as the respondents might not remember their health status accurately.

Part 2: Research questions

Research quesion 1:Research question 1: For the general US population, is there any correlation between the amount of time slept and the number of days the respondent feels full of energy(last 30 days) and if there’s any correlation from this data to the respondent’s sex as well. In the current unhealthy lifestyle of unhealthy diet and irregular sleep cycles, I’m interestd in the respondent’s energy levels.
Variables used:
sleptim1: how much time do you sleep
qlhlth2: how many days full of energy in the past 30 days
sex: respondents sex

Research quesion 2:To find correlation among the physical activity, employment status and income level reagrding the general population in the US. I expect to see direct relation between physical activity and income level or higher employment status of the respondents. But maybe the analysis might lead to some otherwise results and hence the data analysis.
Variables used:
exerany2: exercise in the past 30 days
income2: income level
employ1: employment status

Research quesion 3: To find correlation between general health and overall life satisfaction with correlation extending to the genders. To find noteworthy patterns if people are more health cautious and satisfied.
Variables used:
sex: respondents sex

abcd <- select(brfss2013, qlhlth2 , sex , sleptim1) %>% 
  filter(!is.na(qlhlth2), !is.na(sex), sleptim1 <= 14 )
abcd %>% group_by(qlhlth2) %>%    summary(count=n())
##     qlhlth2          sex         sleptim1     
##  Min.   : 0.00   Male  :163   Min.   : 2.000  
##  1st Qu.: 2.00   Female:287   1st Qu.: 6.000  
##  Median :15.00                Median : 7.000  
##  Mean   :15.59                Mean   : 7.029  
##  3rd Qu.:28.00                3rd Qu.: 8.000  
##  Max.   :30.00                Max.   :14.000
ggplot(data = abcd, aes(x = sleptim1, y = qlhlth2 ))+
    geom_point(shape=1) +    # Use hollow circles
    geom_smooth(method=lm)   +
    scale_x_continuous(limits = c(4,10), breaks = 4:10) +
    facet_grid(. ~  sex) +
    xlab("sleptim1 = How much time do you sleep?") +
    ylab ("qlhlth2: How Many Days Full Of Energy In Past 30 Days")

Survey results indicate that those who sleep between 6 and 8 hours make up the majority of the respondents. There appears to be a positive linear relationship between the x and y variables with a wide dispersion between the number of energetic days reported at each recorded level of sleep (in hours.) The dispersion is greater in male respondents.
